# Release 2026-06-15 — Google sign-in & email verification

## Sign in with Google
- New **Continue with Google** button on both the sign-in and create-account screens. One tap signs
  you in — no password to remember.
- If you already have an account, signing in with Google (same email) **links to your existing
  account**, so all your groups, predictions and points stay exactly where they are.

## Email verification for password accounts
- Creating an account with email + password now sends a **verification link** to your inbox. After
  registering you'll see a "Check your email" screen; click the link to activate your account and
  you're taken straight in.
- Logging in before verifying shows a friendly prompt with a **Resend email** button — or you can
  switch to **Continue with Google** to confirm instantly.
- Verification links expire after 24 hours; request a fresh one any time from the prompt.

## Existing players — what to expect
- The first time you sign in after this update you'll be asked to verify your email (we now require a
  real, owned address). Either click the link we email you, **or** press **Continue with Google** if
  your account email is a Gmail — both keep your Id, points and history intact.

## Technical
- **No third-party auth service / no Firebase.** The backend continues to issue its own JWT; Google
  and email/password are just login methods. All user data stays in Postgres.
- `User` entity gained `GoogleSub` (unique, filtered index), `EmailVerified`, `EmailVerificationToken`
  + `EmailVerificationTokenExpiresAt`; `PasswordHash` is now **nullable** (Google-only users have none).
  Factory `User.FromGoogle(...)` and methods `LinkGoogle`, `MarkEmailVerified`, `SetEmailVerificationToken`.
- Migration `AddGoogleAuthAndEmailVerification` (auto-applies on startup). Existing rows default to
  `email_verified = false` → they must verify or link Google on next sign-in.
- Google ID-token verification via `Google.Apis.Auth` (`GoogleTokenVerifier`, validates audience =
  `Google__ClientId`). New `IEmailSender` → `ResendEmailSender` (Resend REST API via a typed
  `HttpClient`, no SDK; logs the link instead of sending when `Resend__ApiKey` is empty — handy in dev).
- New use cases: `GoogleSignIn` (link-by-verified-email → preserve Id, else provision), `VerifyEmail`
  (consumes token, issues JWT to auto-login), `ResendVerification` (always-200 to avoid email
  enumeration). `RegisterUser` now creates an unverified user + sends the email (no auto-login);
  `LoginUser` throws `EmailNotVerifiedException` → `403 { code: "email_not_verified" }`.
- Account linking for **mismatched emails** (e.g. switching a fake/typo email to a real Google one):
  `LinkGoogleAccount` use case attaches the Google identity to an *existing* account (Id/points kept)
  and **updates that account's email to the Google address**. Identity is proven either by JWT
  (`POST /auth/google/link`, `[Authorize]` — used by the blocking verify modal) or by valid
  email+password (`POST /auth/google/link-credentials` — used by the login "unverified" panel).
  Conflicts (Google already linked elsewhere / email taken) return 409. `User.ChangeEmail` added.
  Frontend `GoogleSignInButton` gained an `onCredential` override so the same button drives sign-in
  vs. linking; `api/auth.ts` adds `linkGoogleAuthed` / `linkGoogleWithCredentials`.
- `AuthController` endpoints: `POST /auth/google`, `POST /auth/google/link`,
  `POST /auth/google/link-credentials`, `POST /auth/verify-email`,
  `POST /auth/resend-verification` (all under the existing `auth` rate-limit policy).
  `IUserRepository.GetByGoogleSubAsync` / `GetByEmailVerificationTokenAsync` added.
- Config: backend `Google__ClientId`, `Frontend__BaseUrl`, `Resend__ApiKey`, `Email__FromAddress/FromName`; frontend `VITE_GOOGLE_CLIENT_ID`.
- Frontend: `@react-oauth/google` with `GoogleOAuthProvider` in `main.tsx`; shared
  `components/GoogleSignInButton.tsx`; new `pages/VerifyEmailPage.tsx` (route `/verify-email`);
  Login/Register reworked with Google button, verify prompts and resend; `api/auth.ts` gains
  `googleSignIn`/`verifyEmail`/`resendVerification` and a new `RegisterResponse` shape.
- See `DEPLOY.md` §2b for Google Cloud Console + SMTP setup.
